## Problem

My son had outgrown his Power Wheels performance, but commercial upgrades were overpriced and poorly designed. I wanted to give him something faster and more reliable while teaching myself about DC electrical systems and safety design.

## Approach

Designed a complete electrical system upgrade using higher-capacity batteries, properly sized conductors, and electronic speed control. This wasn't just about making it faster — it was about doing it right, with proper safety margins and thermal management.

## Key features

- Upgraded from 12V to 24V with careful thermal management in plastic enclosures
- PWM speed controllers for smooth acceleration instead of jarring on/off operation
- Overcurrent protection, thermal cutoffs, and emergency stop systems
- Heavy-gauge cables properly sized for increased current load with professional-grade terminations

## Challenges

Calculating proper conductor sizing for the increased current draw was critical — undersized wires would create dangerous heat buildup. I had to design thermal management solutions that worked within the existing plastic enclosures while maintaining child-safe operation. The safety systems needed to be bulletproof because kids don't follow operating procedures.

## Outcome

The system delivered a 3x speed increase with maintained safety margins and has operated reliably for over two years. It sparked my son's interest in electronics and became a template for similar projects in the neighborhood — several other parents have asked me to help with their kids' rides.
