## Problem

My ATV needed major engine work, but instead of just fixing what was broken, I wanted to understand every component and add performance improvements. This became a complete teardown and rebuild with upgraded internals.

## Approach

I completely disassembled the engine, inspected every component, and rebuilt it with performance upgrades including a new camshaft profile, larger carburetor, and high-compression piston. The goal was both better performance and deeper mechanical understanding.

## Key features

- Complete engine teardown with component-level inspection
- Upgraded camshaft profile for improved power delivery
- Larger carburetor with complete rejetting for new air/fuel requirements
- High-compression piston for increased output
- Documented every step for future maintenance reference

## Challenges

Precise timing was critical for the upgraded camshaft, and the carburetor required complete rejetting for the new air/fuel requirements. I had to balance performance gains with long-term reliability — it's easy to make something fast, harder to make it fast and dependable.

## Outcome

The rebuilt ATV delivers significantly better performance than stock configuration and has operated reliably for several seasons. Every step was documented, and I developed systematic testing and break-in procedures. The project gave me confidence to tackle mechanical problems systematically and help others with similar challenges.
