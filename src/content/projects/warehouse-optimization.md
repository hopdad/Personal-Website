## Problem

Outbound dock operations at Meijer Distribution were creating bottlenecks in our time-critical cold environment. With 50+ store routes depending on our performance, inefficiencies directly impacted customer satisfaction across Michigan and Ohio.

## Approach

I partnered with leadership and the Six Sigma Lean team to completely map our workflow, identify bottlenecks, and redesign material flow patterns. This wasn't about working harder — it was about working smarter in a demanding physical environment.

## Key features

- Complete workflow mapping to identify wasted motion and redundant handling
- Redesigned loading patterns optimized for route delivery sequence
- Onboarded 5-person team on new procedures with emphasis on safety protocols
- Built KPIs to monitor throughput and maintain continuous improvement

## Challenges

Implementing process changes in a live, time-critical cold environment meant we couldn't afford downtime for testing. Every change had to be validated incrementally while maintaining output targets. Getting buy-in from the team required demonstrating results early and incorporating their frontline knowledge into the redesign.

## Outcome

The redesigned process eliminated multiple redundant material handling steps and optimized loading sequence to match route delivery order. We reduced dock-to-truck time by 23% while maintaining our zero-incident safety record during the entire transition period. These improvements became standard practice across all shifts and have been maintained for over 6 years.
